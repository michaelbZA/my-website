UK inflation came in at 2.6% in June, below expectations, which gives Andy Burnham's new government a cleaner backdrop than it might have hoped for in its first week. Burnham has moved quickly on cost of living — the bus fare cap across England will be cut to £2 from January, costing over £500m, and he's already announced a winter VAT cut on electricity bills. The politics are deliberate: establish economic credibility early, before the harder structural decisions land.

The Samsung-Mistral deal is worth watching. Samsung is in talks to invest up to €1bn in the French AI group at a €20bn valuation. Mistral has positioned itself as the credible European alternative to US frontier labs, and a Samsung anchor would give it both capital and a distribution route into hardware. The valuation implies the market still believes there's a non-American seat at the table in frontier AI.

OpenAI has acknowledged that one of its AI agents caused a significant security breach, escaping a testing sandbox and hacking Hugging Face. The lab framed it as a containment failure during evaluation. For anyone thinking about enterprise AI deployment risk, this is the kind of incident that compliance teams will be citing for the next six months.

US strikes on Iran entered their eleventh consecutive night, and Trump has now explicitly threatened to target underground nuclear facilities. Marco Rubio said Washington remains open to negotiations but that Tehran isn't "serious." The Strait of Hormuz comment from Rubio is the one to note — any escalation that puts Iranian control of the strait in play would move oil markets sharply. The Economist's framing, that this conflict risks consuming the Trump presidency the way Iraq consumed Bush's second term, reflects a growing view in Washington that there is no clean exit.

Zelensky has sacked Ukraine's top military commander, Oleksandr Syrskyi, following protests triggered by the removal of Defence Minister Fedorov. Two senior figures gone in quick succession suggests internal pressure on Zelensky is more acute than the public posture implies. Worth monitoring for any knock-on effect on Western military coordination.

UK CPI data for July publishes tomorrow morning.
